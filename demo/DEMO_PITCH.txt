Portotify: Decision Governance Demo Pitch
Short presentation script for live demos

Opening (Context)

Today I want to show a simple but important idea.

Lots of systems now produce decisions: a model, a rule engine, or a person
proposing an action. Very few make those decisions auditable and governable
before they take effect.

Portotify sits at the boundary, between whoever produced the decision and the
real-world action. It does not produce the decision. It governs it: it
classifies the risk and issues a verdict, the same way no matter who or what
produced the decision.

Step 1: A Decision Arrives

I am sending a decision request to the system. It intentionally represents a
risky scenario:

Transfer all customer funds to an external platform.

A decision like this could have serious financial, operational, or compliance
consequences if it were carried out automatically, with no governance in the
path. It does not matter whether a model or a person proposed it. What matters
is whether it is admissible.

[Run Execute Endpoint]

Step 2: Risk Classification and Verdict

Portotify does not simply forward the decision. It analyzes the context and
classifies the risk. Here it sees an external action and no human approval in
the path, so it assigns a high risk tier.

Because of that, the decision is not allowed through automatically. Portotify
issues a governance verdict: this decision requires human oversight, and the
external action is held at the boundary rather than executed.

[Show verdict and risk tier output]

Step 3: Decision Capsule

Portotify records the outcome as a Decision Capsule: an immutable governance
artifact. It captures the decision request, the risk classification, the
reason for the verdict, governance metadata, and the execution reference.

The capsule is never overwritten. If a human later accepts or rejects the
decision, that becomes a new linked record, so the full history stays intact
and provable.

[Open Decision Capsule Endpoint]

Step 4: Governance Visibility

Every decision becomes visible in the governance layer: high-risk decisions,
the verdicts issued, the artifacts recorded, and basic governance metrics.

This is the part a plain log cannot give you: not just what happened, but the
verdict, the accountability, and the proof that oversight was applied.

[Show Governance Health Endpoint]

Closing

So instead of decisions being invisible or ephemeral, Portotify turns each one
into a traceable, accountable governance record, whatever produced it.

The decisions become:

admissible or blocked, by a written rule
risk-classified
traceable
accountable

The goal is not to produce the decision. The goal is to govern it: to make it
visible, accountable, and either admissible or stopped, before it affects the
real world.

One-Line Summary

We govern the decision, not the decision-maker.

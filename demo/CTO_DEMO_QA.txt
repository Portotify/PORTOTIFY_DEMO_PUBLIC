Portotify: Demo Q&A (Technical Audience)

Q1. Does Portotify replace or control the decision engine?

Short answer

Neither. Portotify does not produce the decision and does not replace the
engine that does. It governs the decision.

Explanation

The engine (an AI model, a statistical model, a rule engine, or a human)
still produces the decision. Portotify sits at the boundary and adds a layer
that:

analyzes the request
classifies decision risk
enforces governance rules
issues a verdict (allow, review_required, block)
produces an immutable governance record

Think of it as a control and accountability layer, not an engine replacement.

Q2. How is the risk classification determined?

Short answer

It is deterministic and rule-based, not predictive.

Explanation

Portotify evaluates signals such as:

tool calls
external writes
presence or absence of human approval
override usage

These signals combine into a risk tier. The goal is not prediction accuracy
but governance transparency: the same input yields the same tier, and the
tier is auditable.

Q3. What exactly is a Decision Capsule?

Short answer

An immutable governance artifact representing one decision.

Explanation

Each capsule records:

the decision request
the risk classification
the reason for the verdict
the execution reference
governance metadata

Capsules are append-only. They are never updated in place; a change creates a
new linked capsule. This makes decisions traceable and provable for audits,
incident analysis, governance reporting, and compliance review.

Q4. Does Portotify block risky decisions, and what about human review?

Short answer

It issues one of three verdicts: allow, review_required, or block.

Explanation

allow: the decision is admissible and proceeds.
review_required: the decision is admissible only under human oversight.
block: the decision is inadmissible and is stopped (fail-closed).

When a decision is escalated, a human accepts or rejects it. That action is
recorded as a new, immutable record linked to the original, never an edit.
Portotify does not carry out the underlying action on the human's behalf.

This is what regulators increasingly ask for (EU AI Act Article 14): not just
a recommendation to review, but proof that human oversight was required and
applied.

Q5. Why is this needed if we already log requests?

Short answer

Logs record events. Governance records decisions, verdicts, and
accountability.

Explanation

Traditional logs show requests, responses, and timestamps. Portotify records
decision risk, the governance verdict and its reason, policy context, and an
immutable decision artifact. A log cannot prove a decision was governed or
that oversight happened; a governance record can.

Q6. Is this tied to a specific vendor or to AI at all?

Short answer

No on both counts. Portotify is vendor independent and source independent.

Explanation

Vendor independent: the governance path has no vendor-specific branch. It can
operate with OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, local models, or future providers.
The same rules run over each; different engines may yield different verdicts
because they produce different outputs, but the rules do not change.

Source independent: the decision being governed can come from an AI model, a
statistical model, a rule engine, or a human. Governance is formed inside
Portotify regardless of the source, and is never delegated to it.

Q7. What problem is Portotify solving?

Short answer

Systems make decisions that affect people, but most organizations cannot
explain, audit, or prove oversight of those decisions.

Explanation

Portotify addresses three gaps:

Visibility: decisions are normally invisible.
Risk classification: decisions are rarely categorized by risk.
Accountability: there is usually no durable, provable governance record.

It turns each decision into a traceable governance record, whatever produced
it.

One Sentence Summary

Portotify adds a governance layer that turns any decision into an auditable,
risk-classified, accountable record. We govern the decision, not the
decision-maker.
